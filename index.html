掼蛋 · Partnership Card Game

Guandan

Two decks, four players, two partnerships — race your team from level 2 to Ace by finishing hands first.

Overview Ranking Combos Tribute Playing Scoring
01 — Setup

Overview

Guandan ("throwing eggs") is played by four people in two fixed partnerships, partners seated across from one another. The whole deck is dealt out, and the aim is simple: empty your hand before your opponents do.

Players & Teams

4 players, 2 teams of 2. Partners sit opposite each other at the table.

The Deck

Two standard 54-card decks (with jokers) combined — 108 cards total, dealt entirely so each player holds 27.

The Goal

Be the first team to climb every level, from 2 up to Ace, and then win the deciding hand at Ace level.

The level card is whatever rank your team is currently on — shown here as 5. Every hand, all copies of that rank become wild: they rank just under the jokers, and slot into their natural numeric position within straights, tubes, or plates. As your team advances, the wild rank moves up the track.
The Hearts copy is the true wildcard. Of the copies of the level card, the one in Hearts (♥) can stand in for any card, of any rank or suit, to complete whatever hand you're building — the only cards it can't imitate are the two jokers.
Jokers are always the two highest cards in the game — Small Joker, then Big Joker above it — regardless of level.

Combinations

Every play must be one of these shapes. To beat the previous play, you need a higher combo of the same shape — except bombs, which can land on anything.

A bomb can be played on top of any combo, of any shape, at any time — it's the one way to break another player's control of a trick. Bigger bombs beat smaller bombs, and the joker bomb beats everything.
Where the straight flush sits: it beats an ordinary bomb of 4 or 5 cards, but a bomb of 6 or more cards beats it. Full order, low to high: 4-card bomb → 5-card bomb → straight flush → 6-card-or-larger bomb → joker bomb.
04 — Before the Hand

Tribute

How the previous hand ended decides who owes tribute before the next one begins.

1

Double-down finish

If one team's players finished 1st and 2nd, both losing players must each hand over their single highest card to the winning team.

2

Return cards

In exchange, the winning players give back their lowest non-wild cards, so nobody loses cards on net.

3

Anti-tribute

If the two losing players together hold both jokers, they may refuse to pay tribute at all.

4

Who leads

Whoever owes the largest tribute typically leads the first trick of the new hand.

05 — During the Hand

Playing a Hand

Play moves around the table in turns, each player either beating the last combo or passing.

1

Lead

The designated player opens the trick with any legal combo.

2

Respond

Each player in turn plays a higher combo of the same shape, drops a bomb, or passes.

3

Trick ends

Once everyone else passes, the last player to have played wins the trick and leads the next one, with any combo they choose.

4

Finishing

A player who empties their hand locks in their finish position and sits out for the rest of the hand.

5

Hand ends

Once three players have finished, the fourth is automatically last place and the hand is over.

06 — Advancing

Scoring & Levels

Your team's level gain depends entirely on where your two players land in the finishing order.
